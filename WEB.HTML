TOURISM IN KENYA

Kenya is well known as a major African tourist hub. From the dry, grassy savannah to its rainforests, it boasts numerous different wild animals. Kenya is among few countries in the world that are home to the famous "big five" animals which include the Elephant, Rhino, Lion, Buffalo and Leopard. Kenya also has several archeological sites which attract thousands of history lovers from all over the world. The country is also along the equator which ensures that it has a hot, tropical climate throughout most of the year, this coupled with the fact that it has a long shoreline makes it a perfect attraction for people who love to tour beaches.

In addition, Kenya has plenty of beautiful features which attract more tourists and examples of this include Ngong Hills, Fort Jesus and Gedi ruins. Kenya has many tourist attractions ranging from the sandy beaches at the coast, diverse flora and fauna, game parks, cultural heritage, scenic landscapes just to name but a few. The Nairobi National Park is within the boundaries of the City of Nairobi. Also close by is the popular Giraffe Centre where visitors can watch and even feed the giraffes. Other attractions include the National Museum, Amboseli National Park, Lake Nakuru, Hells Gate, Tsavo East and West National Park to mention but a few.
